# Domani SEO Keyword Research - March 2026

## Executive Summary

This research identifies keyword opportunities for Domani's evening planning productivity app. The "evening planning" niche is notably underserved -- no major app owns this positioning. Most competitors (Sunsama, Todoist, Structured, Motion) position around "daily planner" or "time blocking" rather than evening-specific planning. This creates a significant opportunity for Domani to own the evening planning category.

## Quick Win Opportunities (Lower Competition, High Relevance)

These keywords have low competition and high relevance to Domani's positioning. They can rank within 2-4 months with good content.

### 1. "Evening planning app" - BEST OPPORTUNITY
- **Why**: No app specifically owns this term. Search results return blog posts about the concept, not actual apps.
- **Action**: Optimize homepage title/meta for this phrase. Create a dedicated landing page.
- **Expected difficulty**: Low. Domani could rank page 1 within 2-3 months.

### 2. "Plan tomorrow tonight"
- **Why**: Only one small app blog (malife.app) targets this exact phrase. Domani's tagline matches perfectly.
- **Action**: This should be in the H1, page title, and URL structure. Create a blog post expanding on the concept.
- **Expected difficulty**: Very low. Brand-building opportunity.

### 3. "Ivy Lee method app"
- **Why**: The Ivy Lee method (plan 6 tasks the night before) has strong informational search volume via James Clear's article, but no app specifically claims this methodology.
- **Action**: Blog post: "The Ivy Lee Method, Modernized: How Domani Brings a 100-Year-Old Productivity Hack to Your Phone"
- **Expected difficulty**: Low.

### 4. "Reduce morning decision fatigue"
- **Why**: Problem-aware keyword with low commercial competition. Results are dominated by health/wellness blogs, not apps.
- **Action**: Blog post + landing page section addressing this pain point directly.
- **Expected difficulty**: Low-Medium.

### 5. "How to stop feeling overwhelmed in the morning"
- **Why**: Emotional, problem-aware query. Current results are generic blog advice. An app-specific solution would stand out.
- **Action**: Blog post: "How to Stop Feeling Overwhelmed Every Morning (The Evening Planning Solution)"
- **Expected difficulty**: Low-Medium.

### 6. "Night before planning routine"
- **Why**: Niche phrase with intent that perfectly matches Domani. Current results are personal blogs and Medium articles.
- **Action**: Create definitive guide content.
- **Expected difficulty**: Low.

### 7. "Free daily planner app with evening mode"
- **Why**: Ultra-specific long-tail. Zero competition but captures exact buyer intent.
- **Action**: Ensure app store listing and landing page include these terms.
- **Expected difficulty**: Very low.

| Competitor | Positioning | Monthly Price | Evening Planning? | Threat Level |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Sunsama** | Mindful daily planner | $20/mo | Daily shutdown ritual (close) | High |
| **Todoist** | Quick task capture | $5/mo | No specific feature | Medium |
| **Structured** | Visual time blocking | $5/mo (premium) | No specific feature | Medium |
| **Motion** | AI auto-scheduling | $19/mo | No specific feature | Low |
| **Amazing Marvin** | Customizable planner | $8/mo | No specific feature | Low |
| **Tiimo** | Neurodivergent planning | $6/mo | Evening routines (close) | Low |
| **Do It Tomorrow** | Next-day task planning | Free | Yes (closest competitor) | Medium |

**Key insight**: Sunsama's "daily shutdown" feature is the closest competitive positioning, but it costs $20/mo and frames evening planning as a secondary feature. "Do It Tomorrow" is conceptually close but has minimal market presence. Domani has a real opportunity to own the evening planning category.
